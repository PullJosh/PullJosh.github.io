The Windmill Problem

Illustrated by me, Josh Pullen, for my 3Blue1Brown internship application.

Let S be a finite set of at least two points in the plane.

Assume that no three points of S are colinear.

A windmill is a process that starts with a line ℓ going through a single point P ∈ S.

The line rotates clockwise about the pivot P until the first time that the line meets some other point belonging to S.

This point, Q, takes over as the new pivot, and the line rotates clockwise about Q, until it next meets a point of S.

This process continues indefinitely. Show that we can choose a point P in S and a line ℓ going through P such that the resulting windmill uses each point of S as a pivot infinitely many times.
